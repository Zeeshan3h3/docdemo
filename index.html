❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about women's health and our services.

It is recommended to visit a gynecologist at least once a year for a routine checkup. If you are pregnant, experiencing irregular periods, or have any concerns about your reproductive health, you should schedule visits more frequently as advised by the doctor.
Common symptoms of PCOS include irregular or missed periods, excessive hair growth (hirsutism), acne, weight gain, thinning hair on the scalp, and difficulty getting pregnant. If you notice any of these symptoms, consult a gynecologist for proper diagnosis and treatment.
You should ideally start prenatal care as soon as you find out you're pregnant or when you're planning to conceive. Early prenatal care helps monitor the health of both mother and baby and can help identify and address any complications early on.
During your first visit, the doctor will discuss your medical history, menstrual cycle, and any health concerns. A physical examination may be conducted based on your symptoms. The visit is confidential and comfortable — our goal is to ensure you feel at ease throughout the consultation.
Mild cramps during periods are common, but severe pain that interferes with daily activities is not normal. Conditions like endometriosis, fibroids, or PCOS can cause intense menstrual pain. If your periods are very painful, consult a gynecologist for evaluation and treatment options.
